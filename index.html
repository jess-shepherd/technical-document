How to drive a manual transmission.

THE STEP BY STEP

Written by wetshep, Feb 8, 2021

Step 1: Buckle up Buttercup

This is the MOST IMPORTANT STEP OF ALL so you better click it, or you're putting yourself at unecessary risk.

Step 2: Observe your gear shifter

Some shifters operate differently. Some have fewer gears, more gears, different placement for the gears, and reverse as well. Take a good long look at your shifter and take a mental note on it's layout, as you'll need to quickly shift between gears while in motion. (preferably not looking at the stick while you stift)

Step 3: Crank your Ride

Wiggle the stick around to make sure it's in neutral, then with your left foot hold down on the clutch (all the way), and with your right foot hold the brake. Then hit the button or turn the key.

Step 4: Put it in 1st gear

Still holding the clutch and brake firmly... follow the chart on the stick and find first gear. (and do be sure your parking brake is off)

You found first? Great. Now here's the fun part: Let off the brake then SLOWLY release the clutch as you SLOWLY apply an equal amount of pressure to the gas pedal.

Now either one of two things happened... you stalled the vehicle or you're off!

If you stalled, (don't panic) just reapply pressure to the break and the gas and turn the key or hit the button again to restart the vehicle. Just keep doing step 4 until you're successful, then head over to Step 5.

Step 5:

Okay, so you're on the move; but you should'nt stay in 1st gear for long, it's just to get you started. Once your RPM's hit about 2500, it's it's time to shift.

So, while in motion at 2500 RPM, let off the gas, and then press down on the clutch all the way, and swing the stick down into 2nd. Once you're there, slowly let of the clutch as you slowly apply pressure to the gas pedal, same as before.

Step 6: Know your timing

To keep your engine/transmission as healthy as possible, you should shift gears between 2500-2700 RMP. No matter how fast you're going you want to try and keep the RPM gauge low, or you're overworking the engine. So shift at 2500 RPM. It doesn't matter if you're in 2nd going to 3rd, or 5th going to 6th, shift at 2500.

Step 7: Down-shifting

Time to slow down? Let off the gas and and slowly use the break when necessary. But down shift at the same rate that you shifted up. So if you're going 55 in 4th gear, but need to slow down, hit the clutch and swing to 3rd and slowly let off the clutch as you operate your breaks as necessary. Down shifting also slows the car down on it's own. Eventually you'll work your way down to 1st again, which is the gear you want to be stopped in. You can't start up and go from any gear other than 1st, or you'll stall.

Turning off the vehicle

When stopped, you'll have the clutch held down, so put the car in to neutral. (that's the wiggly middle zone of the stick shifter) and also your "park" I'd advise hitting the parking break just for good measure. Then you can power down and hop out.


Well that's pretty much all there is to it. I hope reverse speaks for itself to you. One thing I could warn is don't give the engine too much gas when your stopped in less than favorable conditions, you could fishtail and spin out of control and put yourself and other in harm's way... so if you're dying to try and race or do a burnout, do it when it's dry and sunny and you're on good pavement.
